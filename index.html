Professional Profile

I am Mahapatra Ashish Godabrisha, a passionate researcher and conservationist with an academic background in Marine Biology and professional experience in biodiversity research, community outreach, and conservation documentation. My academic journey laid the foundation for my interest in the biological sciences, which was further strengthened through field-based experiences with some of India's premier biodiversity organizations.

Academic Background

My fascination with marine and wildlife ecosystems led me to pursue formal education in Marine Biology, where I received training in ecological research, species identification, field survey techniques, and marine conservation strategies.

Professional Journey

I began my career as a Consultant with the Odisha Biodiversity Board, working on biodiversity documentation and community involvement. Later, I served as a Project Associate at the Zoological Survey of India, focusing on ecological and faunal surveys.

Field Experience

Participated in turtle nesting monitoring and awareness programs in coastal Odisha, particularly during the Olive Ridley turtle mass nesting (arribada) events.

Vision

I aim to remain at the intersection of science and community, where traditional wisdom converges with modern ecological knowledge. My long-term goal is to contribute to marine biodiversity conservation and coastal ecosystem resilience.
